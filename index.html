Sean's website!

Hi! I'm Sean and this is my personal site that I made for the Galvanize pre-course project. Above is a .gif of a Terminator crushing the opposition, which is how I expect my journey towards becoming a web developer to go (with the help of Galvanize, of course!)

First, a little about myself:

I was originally born in the great state of Texas but only lived there for a little over a year before my parents drove me across the country to Clinton, MA. Here's a picture of the awesome house that I grew up in!

After growing up in a great town and doing normal kid things I decided to go to school at Northeastern University in Boston. I majored in Music Industry (think "Music Business") with a minor in Business and afterwards joined a band and started touring the country!

After I got tired of being a rock star and paying obscene amounts of rent in Boston, I decided to move to a new city and see where life would take me. The Space Needle was calling and it didn't take much to convince me that Seattle would be a great place to call my home for the next stage.

So I got myself a full time job as an Audio-Visual technician, which was great at first because I didn't know anybody in Seattle and having a steady source of income was a pretty convenient thing to have. We were mainly responsible for rental gigs which required me to drive all around the Puget Sound area - even all the way out to Spokane and the Olympic Peninsula on a few occasions - so it was a very good way to become acquainted with the area. On the weekends my girlfriend and I would go hiking and further explore the amazing scenery around us.

But soon I realized that there was very little future in my job and I struggled to find a way to utilize my music/audio/technical skills that didn't involve inconsistent scheduling or consistent night and weekend work. My girlfriend and a good number of my friends were in the tech industry and I always somewhat regretted not learning how to code. We had a "Learn Python The Hard Way" text book lying around the apartment so I took it upon myself to start learning in my free time. I think the first line of code I ever wrote was probably similar to many other programmers:

print "Hello World!"

After about a year of non-committal but steadily progressing study I decided I was having so much fun that I should consider transitioning to a career in tech. I delved into Django, Python's well-regarded web framework and it wasn't long before I was looking at coding bootcamps to further my learning. I did a lot of research but Galvanize was clearly the best fit, so here I am!

The fixed link in the bottom right of the page links to my goals page. Click it to check out what I hope to achieve at Galvanize!
