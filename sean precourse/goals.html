GOALS

I mentioned on the first page that I became interested in programming as part of a solution to change my life for the better. I have several goals that I would like to accomplish, both during the Galvanize program and further into my career:

  • Get a job. This may seem obvious but it’s also very important. I need to pay off my loans somehow!
  • Learn the fundamentals of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript and hone those skills to the point where I become an attractive candidate for tech positions, ideally for companies in creative industries (such as music or film).
  • Learn how to learn new technologies. One of the first things I discovered about this industry is that it is always changing. Being able to adapt to rapidly changing environments and processes will be a huge advantage in what will be a very competitive market and I intend to embrace that concept wholeheartedly.
  • Become a competent, jack-of-all-trades full-stack developer, but also choose a path to specialize in and ultimately master. I do not know what this path will be, whether it’s backend, front end, CSS ninja, or data analytics, but I know that I will be much more valuable as a developer if I can do something better than most.
  • Learn to collaborate. Honestly one of the biggest draws of working in tech for me was the opportunity to collaborate with others on a regular basis. I’ve always thrived in a group environment and I love the exchange of ideas and methods to come up with the best final product.
  • Ultimately make my way back into the music industry. This was touched on before but it’s a big reason I chose to switch careers to Web Development. Tech is now an industry that is prevalent and essential to every other industry. As said before I thrive in collaborative environments and I would love nothing more than to go back to my community not only as a musician/sound engineer but also as a skilled web dev who can provide unique insights to help contribute to worthwhile projects.

That's it for goals (for now). I'm so excited to get started and really delve into this whole new world. I'm very grateful for the opportunity and look forward to seeing what I can accomplish!
